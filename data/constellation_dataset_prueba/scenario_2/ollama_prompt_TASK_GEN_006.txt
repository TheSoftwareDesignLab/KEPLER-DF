Task Request: High-Importance Capture of Indonesia Ecuatorial Landscape
This is a high-priority, urgent operational task requiring a high-resolution optical snapshot of the Indonesian Ecuatorial region. The goal is to capture a clear visual documentation of the landscape using multispectral vegetation analysis and thermal mapping.

Location Context: The target region is Indonesia's Ecuatorial region, encompassing the country's central and eastern parts. Due to the region's diverse geography and climate, a combination of country, city, and landmark details will be used to describe the location.

Mission Allocation Priority: This task is assigned an absolute institutional priority due to its critical nature and the importance of capturing high-resolution visual data for future operational planning and decision-making.

Sensor and Physical Objectives: The primary sensor is VNIR (Vegetation Normalized Difference Index), which enables the capture of multispectral vegetation analysis and thermal mapping. The goal is to assess chlorophyll absorption level, calculate a vegetation NDVI index, and map surface reflectance for accurate classification and monitoring.

Relative Timeframes: The task must be completed within the next three days during the afternoon, specifically overnight. This timeframe allows for optimal conditions for multispectral vegetation analysis and thermal mapping.
